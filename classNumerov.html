Detailed Description

Numerov's method

This class implements Numerov's method using a couple of helper functions. Numerov's method solves a differential equation of the form

\[ y''(x) + g(x)y(x) = 0 \]

The equation is solved by iteratively computing

\[ y_{n+1} = \frac{\left( 2 + \frac{5 h^2}{6} g_n \right) y_n - \left( 1 + \frac{h^2}{12} g_{n-1} \right) y_{n-1}}{\left( 1 + \frac{h^2}{12} g_n \right)} \]

with \( y_n = y(x_n) \) and \( g_n = g(x_n) \). Clearly this equation needs two initial conditions \( y_0 \) and \( y_1 \). We integrate the equation from outside to inside. Since the wavefunction hs to decay to zero at infinity we choose \( y_0 = 0 \). Now depending on the number of knots of wavefunction we decide whether to set \( y_1 = \pm\varepsilon \).
